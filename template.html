ARP Website Accepting Applications

Council Member Bollman wants Buffalo Public Schools to hire new superintendent from within district

Greetings residents of the Lovejoy District,

The City of Buffalo has issued a call to local organizations to apply for American Rescue Plan funding. Food security, job training, the arts, public health, public safety and affordable housing projects all qualify.

As the Common Council liaison for the historic sum of ARP funding flowing into the city, I promised to ensure parity across all nine districts. Non-profits and other groups doing work on-the-ground will help determine the best way for this money to be spent.

Anyone can sign up for funding opportunity notifications when they are made available on the City of Buffalo Website

External organizations with specific issue-expertise should begin developing project proposals to submit. Available funding varies by program and each one is highly competitive. I encourage all community organizations in Lovejoy to consider applying for this funding.

Qualified, well-resourced organizations must conceptualize and implement projects that will advance the economic, social and cultural prospects of the City of Buffalo.

If you have any questions or need assistance with the application process, contact my office at 716-851-5151

Prioritizing Local Talent

Council Member Bollman wants Buffalo Public Schools to hire new superintendent from within district

The federal government will distribute $330 million to the City of Buffalo as part of the American Rescue Plan that was signed into law last year. This influx of funding is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to address some longstanding needs in the Lovejoy District and across the city.

I am honored to be chosen as the Common Council liaison to the administration to help ensure that this money is spent responsibly and equitably across the city.

Council members are the people most in touch with the needs of their districts. Therefore, it is vital the Council have a strong voice in the process of disrtibuting these funds. I am asking all residents of the Lovejoy District to contact my office and make their priorities known.

This money could have a transformational impact on the City of Buffalo and correct mistakes from generations ago that still affect us today. My role is to establish effective, efficient channels of communication between the public, the Common Council and the administration to make this possible.
