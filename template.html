A Message From Council Member Wyatt

Greetings University District Friends,

I hope this newsletter finds you and your family in good spirits. We are more than halfway through winter and spring is just around the corner. Hopefully, this means sunshine, flowers and warmer temperatures are on their way.

The University District embarks on building and strengthening our community through partnerships and information. To date, we have had two successful events. The first was “Town Hall with Buffalo Police Commissioner.” This was an opportunity for the City of Buffalo residents to meet our new Police Commissioner Joseph Gramaglia. In this meeting the community communicated the needs and expectations they want.

We have many activities in the making towards the up-bringing of our community. We would be grateful for your participation. Together we can make the University District a safe place to live, learn, do business, and raise a family!

Responsible Community Food Stores

Council Member Wyatt wants food stores to respect neighborhoods



On March 10 & 16, 2022, Lovejoy District Council Member Bollman and I hosted a Food Store License Renewal where we were able to hear from store owners, the Department of Inspections, the Buffalo Police Department, and the community regarding food stores in the community. Residents deserve corner markets, delis and food stores that reflect their neighborhood in a positive way. They also deserve to be informed of the inspection and law enforcement role in maintaining high quality food stores that serve the community. The meetings we held were very productive and residents made their concerns heard to the business owners. I am proud to say it was a productive, respectful dialogue and the majority of business owners are amenable to the concerns of residents.

There are a few primary areas of concern. Stores cannot be allowed to leave stock and inventory strewn all over the sales floor. The clutter not only looks bad, it presents a danger to customers, especially senior citizens. I will not tolerate poorly maintained stores with trash in front of the building, selling loose cigarettes and drug paraphernalia inside. I would like to continue to see mutual respect between the residents and owners who take pride and invest in the neighborhood as their own. I appreciate the University District’s support in this effort.

Bailey Avenue Streetscape

Huge infrastructure improvements beginning soon



Bailey Avenue has a long-rooted history in the University District. Finally, we are proud to announce that Bailey Avenue Streetscape construction will start in Spring 2023. This is a federally funded project that will cost 6 million dollars. Phase one will begin at Winspear Avenue to Dartmouth Avenue. Phase two will begin at Dartmouth Avenue to Kensington Avenue. The scope of work includes street paving, replacement of sidewalks, new drains, curbs, striping, street lighting system, and public art.

The City of Buffalo & Watts Architecture & Engineering will be hosting a series of community meetings to get your input. With over 50 local businesses, multiple schools, churches, and community centers on Bailey Avenue, Council Member Wyatt will make sure the community’s voice is heard throughout this process. We are looking forward to the new and improved Bailey Avenue that will increase economic development and a renewed pride within the University District.
