April 1, 2022 | Issue 02

A Message From Council Member Rivera

Thank you for reading another edition of the Niagara District Newsletter. I have been encouraged by the reducing number of COVID-19 cases in Buffalo and Erie County as we approach Spring. My office has a staff member physically in City Hall Monday through Thursday for your assistance.

I want to extend my thoughts and prayers to the three Buffalo police officers who were wounded during a chase late last month. I commend them and all the police officers who display bravery and selflessness everyday keeping our city safe.

I look forward to continuing to work hard for you, the residents of the Niagara District. Please reach out to my office or stop in any time you have questions.

Police Advisory Board Accepting Applications Until April 13, 2022

The Buffalo Common Council is seeking volunteer community-minded individuals who have a life story of Personal Restorative Justice to be a part of the newly formed Community Police Advisory Committee. All voices are important when considering Community Policing and the Council believes that individuals who have been on the other side of the law can bring personal experience on dealing with police that can assist this Committee in proposing policy reforms. If you believe you fit this criteria and want to contribute to making Community Policing better in our city, we encourage you to apply today.

HOW TO APPLY:

  • - Google Form
  • - Email resume to councilstaff@buffalony.gov
  • - Submit resume to the City Clerk (65 Niagara Square, 13th floor)
